# Tofu Chili  
  
*Yield: 4 servings*  
**Ingredients:**  
* 1 (14-ounce) package super-firm tofu, drained and pressed (you can use extra-firm if you want, just press it for longer)  
* 1 tablespoon soy sauce  
* 1/2 tablespoon avocado oil  
* 1 tablespoon cornstarch  
* 1 tablespoon olive oil  
* 1 large yellow onion, diced  
* 1 green bell pepper, diced  
* 4 cloves garlic, minced  
* 2 tablespoons chili powder  
* 1 tablespoon cumin  
* 1 teaspoon smoked paprika  
* 1 teaspoon dried oregano  
* ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)  
* 2 (28-ounce) cans crushed tomatoes  
* 2 cups vegetable broth  
* 1 (19-ounce) can kidney beans, drained and rinsed  
* 1 (19-ounce) can black beans, drained and rinsed  
* Salt and pepper, to taste  
**Instructions:**  
1. **Prepare the Tofu Crumbles:**  
    * Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.  
    * In a large bowl, crumble the pressed tofu into small pieces.  
    * Drizzle the tofu with 1/2 tablespoon avocado oil and the soy sauce. (This will prevent the tofu from sticking to the baking sheet)  
    * Add the cornstarch to the tofu, toss to coat. (This will help crisp up the tofu)  
    * Spread the tofu out onto a parchment paper-lined baking sheet, and put aside. Put this in the oven while you’re simmering the chili.  
    * Bake for 35 to 40 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the tofu is browned and chewy.  
    * Remove from the oven and set aside.  
2. **Prepare the Chili:**  
    * In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat.  
    * Add the diced yellow onion and green bell pepper; sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.  
    * Add the minced garlic, 2 tablespoons chili powder, cumin, 1 teaspoon smoked paprika, dried oregano, and cayenne pepper (if using). Stir constantly for about 1 minute until fragrant.  
    * Pour in the crushed tomatoes and vegetable broth; stir to combine.  
    * Add the kidney beans and black beans  
    * Bring to a boil and then reduce to simmer and continue to simmer with the lid off for 1 hour, giving it a stir every now and then. If the chili gets too thick, add water as needed to reach your desired consistency. (While it’s simmering, put the tofu in the oven)  
    * Once the chili has simmered, add the tofu and stir well.   
3. **Serve:**  
    * Serve topped with salsa, cheese, avocado, or even just green onion.
