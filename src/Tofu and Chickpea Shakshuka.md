# Tofu and Chickpea Shakshuka  
  
*Yield: 3 large or 4 smaller servings*  
**Ingredients:**  
* 1 (14-ounce) package super-firm tofu, drained and pressed (you can use extra-firm, just be sure to press it for longer)  
* 2 tablespoons lemon juice (or concentrate)  
* ½ teaspoon ground turmeric  
* 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided  
* 1 medium white onion, diced  
* 3 red bell peppers, diced into larger pieces  
* 3 garlic cloves, minced  
* 2 teaspoons ground cumin  
* 1.5 tablespoons smoked paprika  
* ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)  
* 1 (28-ounce) can crushed fire roasted tomatoes  
* 1 (14-ounce) can crushed tomatoes  
* 1 (15-ounce) can chickpeas, rinsed and drained  
* 5 ounces fresh baby spinach  
* Salt and black pepper, to taste  
* Fresh parsley and cilantro, chopped, for garnish (optional)  
**Instructions:**  
1. **Prepare the Tofu:**  
    * Cut the pressed tofu into cubes.  
    * Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.  
    * Add the tofu cubes and cook until browned on all sides, about 10 minutes.  
    * Drizzle with lemon juice and sprinkle with turmeric, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat.  
    * Remove tofu from the skillet and set aside.  
2. **Prepare the Sauce:**  
    * In the same skillet, heat the remaining 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat.  
    * Add the diced onion and bell peppers; sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.  
    * Add the minced garlic, cumin, paprika, and cayenne pepper (if using); cook for 1 minute until fragrant.  
    * Stir in the crushed tomatoes. Bring to a simmer and cook until the sauce thickens slightly, about 10 minutes.  
    * 2 minutes into the simmer, add the chickpeas.   
3. **Combine and Serve:**  
    * Add the baby spinach to the sauce; cook until wilted, about 2 minutes.  
    * Return the tofu to the skillet; stir to combine and heat through.  
    * Season with salt and black pepper to taste.  
    * Garnish with chopped parsley and cilantro.
