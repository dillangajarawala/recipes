# Lentil Coconut Curry  
  
*Yield: 4 servings*  
**Ingredients:**  
* 1 tablespoon avocado oil, separated into 2 halves  
* 1 (14-ounce) package super-firm tofu, drained and pressed (you can use extra-firm, just be sure to press it for longer)  
* 1 tablespoon cornstarch  
* 1 tablespoon soy sauce  
* 2 teaspoons cumin seeds  
* 2 pcs frozen ginger (you can also use fresh, but this is easier)  
* 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped  
* 1 teaspoon ground turmeric  
* 1 teaspoon ground cumin  
* 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander  
* 1/2 teaspoon garam masala (optional)  
* ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)  
* 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes  
* 1 cup brown lentils, rinsed  
* 2 cups water  
* 1 (14-ounce) can coconut milk  
* 1 teaspoon sea salt  
* Fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish  
**Instructions:**  
1. **Prepare Tofu:**  
    * Preheat the oven to 400F.  
    * Cut the pressed tofu into cubes, and transfer to a bowl  
    * Drizzle the tofu with 1/2 tablespoon avocado oil and the soy sauce. (This will prevent the tofu from sticking to the baking sheet)  
    * Add the cornstarch to the tofu, toss to coat. (This will help crisp up the tofu)  
    * Spread the tofu out onto a parchment paper-lined baking sheet, and put aside. We’ll put this in the oven later.   
2. **Sauté Spices:**  
    * Heat 1/2 tablespoon avocado oil in a large pot over medium heat.  
    * Add the cumin seeds; sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute.  
    * Add the chopped ginger and garlic; cook for another minute.  
3. **Add Spices and Tomatoes:**  
    * Pour in the crushed tomatoes.  
    * Stir in the ground turmeric, cumin, coriander, and garam masala (if using). Stir for about 5 minutes.  
4. **Cook Lentils:**  
    * Add the rinsed lentils and water to the pot. Also add in the cayenne pepper (if using).  
    * Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 40 minutes, or until lentils are tender.  
    * While the lentils are simmering, put the tofu into the oven to bake for 30 minutes. It should finish before the lentils.  
5. **Add Coconut Milk and Tofu:**  
    * After the lentils have finished simmering, Stir in the coconut milk and sea salt. Stir for 1-2 minutes  
    * Add the tofu, simmer for 2 more minutes  
6. **Serve:**  
    * Garnish with chopped fresh cilantro.  
    * Serve hot with rice (I personally like brown jasmine rice)
