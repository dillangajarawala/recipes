# Chickpea Pasta with Zucchini and Mushrooms  
  
*Yield: 3 servings*  
**Ingredients:**  
* 1 tablespoon olive oil  
* 2 medium zucchinis, cut into quarter slices  
* 1 (8-ounce) container of Baby Bella/Crimini mushrooms, washed, stems removed, and sliced  
* 1.5 teaspoons ground black pepper  
* 1.5 teaspoons red pepper flakes  
* 1 teaspoon minced garlic (you could use fresh or jarred, doesn’t matter)  
* 1 (28-ounce) can of crushed tomatoes  
* 1 (14-ounce) can of crushed tomatoes (blending diced tomatoes would also work)  
* 0.5 teaspoon ground oregano  
* 0.5 teaspoon onion powder  
* 0.5 teaspoon garlic powder  
* 0.5 tablespoon soy sauce (olive/caper brine would also work great here)  
* 1 (12-ounce) package chickpea pasta (I really love the Trader Joes one)  
* Red chili flakes, black pepper, and grated parmesan for topping  
**Instructions:**  
1. **Cook the Veggies:**  
    * In a pan, add 3/4 of the olive oil over medium heat  
    * Add the zucchini and mushrooms to the pan, along with 1 teaspoon of ground black pepper and 1 teaspoon of red chili flakes.   
    * Sauté for 5 minutes or until the mushrooms have changed color and the zucchini is a little soft. Remove from the pan and set aside.   
2. **Make the Sauce:**  
    * In the same pan, add the remaining olive oil and the minced garlic. Heat it for about 30 seconds, the pan should still be very hot from the veggies.   
    * Once the garlic is sizzling, add in all the tomatoes, stir to combine.   
    * Add all the remaining spices and the soy sauce, stir to combine.   
    * Bring the sauce to a low boil and simmer for 20 minutes (you’ll want to cover it for this whole part)  
3. **Boil the Pasta:**  
    * While the sauce is simmering, bring a pot of water to a boil.   
    * Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions. Drain the pasta and reserve some pasta water. (Usually for chickpea pasta it takes less time so I like to start this towards the end of the sauce simmering time)  
4. **Combine Ingredients:**  
    * Once the sauce is done simmering, add the pasta to the pan and the veggies from before. Stir to combine thoroughly. If it’s too thick, add some pasta water to thin it out.   
5. **Serve:**  
    * Serve the pasta topped with extra red chili flakes and black pepper as well as grated parmesan.
